Pollution, What is it?

Pollution is the releasing of substances into the environment in amounts that can cause harmful effects and which natural biological processes cannot easily remove. It is the contamination of an environment by a sustance - known as a pollutant - that reduces its quality in some way, shape or form.

What are pollutants and why are they bad?

Pollutants are substances introduced into an environment which have undesired effects and/or adversely impacts the usefulnes of a resource. These pollutants damage the environment in some aspect with the potential to harm the human, animal or plant life present. Pollutants can be foreign man made substances - and usually they are - or in some cases naturally produced substances with potential to cause harm.

Types of Pollutants

While there are many possible ways to classify pollutants, most pollutants fall into two major categories:

  • Biodegradable Pollutants
    • Pollutants which can be broken down into simpler, harmless substances naturally in due time by decomposers and micro-organisms like bacteria and fungi.

  • Non-Biodegradable Pollutants
    • This is the opposite of a biodegradable pollutat, these are pollutants which cannot be broken down and decomposed naturally or pollutants which take very long to be decomposed naturally.

Types of Pollution

There are many types of pollution but there are roughly seven main types which have the largest impact.
These types are as follows:

  1. Land Pollution
  2. Water Pollution
  3. Air Pollution
  4. Light Pollution
  5. Noise Pollution
  6. Thermal Pollution
  7. Radioacive Pollution

Each Type of pollution mentioned has its own specific causes and effects with individualized solutions but something each has in common with the others is that they all cause harm to the Earth and its inhabitents.


Pollution has been a problem for a long time but with the advent of the Industrial Revolution it has increased exponentially. Due to the excessive amounts of pollution several of Earth's resources have become contaminated. If this continues soon the earth will be irreparably damaged which would not spell good things for humans and the other occupants of this planet. Pollution contributes significantly to global warming, destruction of the ozone layer and several diseases like cancer.
